Labor Market Tightness Dashboard

This dashboard compares the unemployment rate with estimates of the natural rate of unemployment. It presents two views: a credible-set approach using the Kansas City Fed estimate and a standardized-gap approach using both the Kansas City Fed estimate and the CBO benchmark.

Interpretation notes

Method notes

KC Fed credible-set approach: We compute the unemployment gap as unemployment minus the KC Fed estimate of u*. We then compute a lower and upper gap using the upper and lower KC Fed credible bounds. If zero lies inside that gap band, the labor market is labeled balanced. If the whole band is above zero, the labor market is labeled slack. If the whole band is below zero, the labor market is labeled tight.

Standardized-gap approach: We compute the unemployment gap and divide it by the time-series standard deviation of the relevant natural-rate estimate. This is a descriptive normalization based on historical variability, not a measure of statistical significance.

Monthly alignment: The CBO series is quarterly. In this dashboard, each quarterly CBO value is repeated across the three months in the same quarter so it can be compared with monthly unemployment data.
